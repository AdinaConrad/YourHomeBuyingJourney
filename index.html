Step 1
Get pre-approved
📍 You are here
Talk to a lender before you start touring. Knowing your budget keeps you focused — and makes your offer stronger when the right house comes along.
💰 Money & action items
  • ✓Gather pay stubs, tax returns (2 yrs), and bank statements to give your lender.
  • $No money due yet — but know your down payment amount and where it's sitting.
Step 2
House hunting
📍 You are here
Tour homes with your agent. Take notes, photos, and pay attention to how each home feels. We'll narrow it down together until you find the one.
Step 3
Submit an offer
📍 You are here
Your agent writes the offer — price, terms, deposit amount, and any contingencies. We submit it to the seller and wait for their response.
💰 Money & action items
  • !Your contract will specify 3 deposit amounts, their due dates, form of payment, and where to deliver them. Review this carefully.
  • $Deposit 1 (initial deposit) is typically due within days of a fully signed contract. Check your contract for the exact amount, form, and delivery location.
Step 4
Offer accepted 🎉
📍 You are here
Both sides sign the contract. Big moment — but the clock is now running on several deadlines. Read everything.
💰 Money & action items
  • $Deposit 1 may be due now or very shortly. Confirm the dollar amount, form of payment, and where to deliver it — all in your contract.
  • ✓Forward the signed contract to your lender immediately so they can begin the loan process.
  • ✓Contact your attorney — attorney review begins now.
NJ specific
Step 5
Attorney review
📍 You are here
New Jersey law gives both parties 3 business days to review the contract with an attorney. Changes can be negotiated, and either party can cancel during this window. This is totally normal — don't panic.
⚠️ Action items — do these now
  • !Read the full contract — carefully. If anything is unclear, ask your attorney before this window closes. Once attorney review ends, you are bound.
  • !Review the seller's disclosure. This tells you everything the seller knows about the home's condition — water damage, repairs, past issues. Read every line.
  • !Review HOA documents (if applicable). Understand the rules, fees, and restrictions before you commit.
  • $Locate all 3 deposit requirements in your contract. Write down the amount, form of payment (personal check, certified check, wire, etc.), due date, and where to deliver each one.
  • $Deposit 2 is typically due at the end of attorney review or shortly after. Confirm in your contract.
  • ✓Your home inspection is scheduled — confirm the date, time, and plan to attend in person.
Step 6
Home inspection
📍 You are here
A licensed inspector checks the home top to bottom — structure, systems, roof, and more. Shore properties often require additional checks for flood, septic, or HVAC.
💰 Money & action items
  • $Inspection fee paid directly to the inspector at time of inspection (~$400–$700, varies by size).
  • $Deposit 2 may be due around this time if not already delivered. Check your contract deadline.
  • ✓Attend the inspection. Walk through with the inspector and ask questions.
  • !After the report, you may negotiate repairs or credits with the seller. Your agent and attorney will guide you.
  • ✓Start getting homeowners insurance quotes now. Contact at least 2–3 insurance providers to compare coverage and rates. Your lender will require proof of insurance before closing — don't wait on this.
Step 7
Mortgage application & appraisal
📍 You are here
Your lender officially processes the loan. The home is independently appraised to confirm its value. Stay patient — this stage typically takes 3–5 weeks.
💰 Money & action items
  • $Appraisal fee paid upfront to your lender (~$500–$700). They will let you know when it's due.
  • $Deposit 3 may be due during this period. Confirm the deadline, amount, form, and delivery in your contract.
  • !Start setting aside closing costs now. NJ closing costs typically run 2–4% of purchase price on top of your down payment. Exact figures come from your title company and lender closer to closing.
  • ✓Respond to any lender document requests quickly — delays push your closing date.
  • !Secure your homeowners insurance policy. Choose your provider and bind the policy — your lender will need proof of insurance (a binder letter) before they can issue clear to close. For Shore properties, also confirm whether you need separate flood insurance.
Step 8
Clear to close
📍 You are here
Your lender approves the loan — this is called "clear to close." A final walkthrough is scheduled. Settlement is right around the corner.
💰 Money & action items
  • $Review your Closing Disclosure from your lender carefully — it shows final loan terms and exact closing costs.
  • $Your title company will send wire instructions for cash due at closing. Wire at least 24 hours before. Always confirm wire instructions by phone — never trust email alone.
  • ✓Do your final walkthrough and confirm the home is in agreed-upon condition.
  • ✓Confirm closing time and location with your attorney.
  • ✓Schedule your utility transfers. Contact each utility provider (electric, gas, water, trash, internet) to transfer service into your name effective on your closing date. Don't wait until after closing — some providers need several days' notice.
Step 9
Settlement — closing day 🔑
📍 You are here
Sign the paperwork, funds are transferred, and the deed is recorded. Congratulations — you get the keys. Welcome to the Jersey Shore.
💰 Money & action items
  • $Confirm your wire has been received by the title company before you arrive.
  • ✓Bring a valid photo ID. Your attorney will walk you through every document you sign.
  • ✓Once everything is signed and funds are disbursed, the deed is recorded — and the home is yours.
  • ✓Confirm your utilities are active in your name as of today.
